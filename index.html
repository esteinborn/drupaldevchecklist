Purpose of this Checklist

There are reasons why things can not done. Writing up a drupal development checklist has proven to be a fairly complex task. While I have been learning Drupal, I have been trying to wrap my head around all the related technologies (ie. SASS, GIT) and "the proper ways" of doing things, I knew I would need a resource like this website. And since there are people learning this stuff everyday, the goal is for this drupal development checklist to be a valuable resource that anybody can reference to learn Drupal. The Web Dev Checklist is an amazing tool and the inspiration for building this Drupal Dev Checklist. I am currently focused on Drupal 7, it will move to Drupal 8 eventually.

About Me

I have been developing websites for the New York Department of State for 11 years, and recently, with some internal reorganization efforts, I have been moved to a group that will be rolling out Drupal Websites for agencies that are part of our reorganization. This list will be the contributed works of (hopefully) many people, but I owe Christopher Catalina, Eric Steinborn, Valerie Perrotte, The Albany Drupal group and my fellow coworkers in the GGC Content Management Cluster a tremendous amount of thanks since their contributions helped me get this together.
